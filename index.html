The Nativity Façade (1894 - 1930)

On the east facing side lies the original Nativity façade–the first façade ever constructed for the Basilica de la Sagrada Família.

In accordance with the chronology of construction, this façade represents the birth and life of Jesus in terms of events, with its many Nativity scenes and various decorative elements symbolising nature.

It is divided into 3 distinct portals, each representing a different virtue and member of the Holy Family, and contains 4 bell towers representing the apostles Barnabas, Simon, Jude, and Matthias (when viewing them from left to right).

It is the only façade whose construction began in Antoni Gaudí’s lifetime. Read more about the Nativity façade here.
